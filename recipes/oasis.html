Oasis sought out to be a less goofy version of the Beatles. Noel basically used classic blues guitar played in standard rock rhythm patterns and stole lots of cool riffs from 70s bands like T Rex, Bowie, and obviously the Beatles.

  • Use 2 Gibson or Epiphone guitars, one playing syncopated rhythm and one playing blues leads and simple arpeggios, with a marshall amp and any bass.
  • Effect pedals are kept to a minimum, just use a Rat distortion pedal and lots of gain on the amp
  • Keep the drums simple and use fills to mark transitions
  • Use a major key with some "wrong" chords to make it interesting. i.e. if playing in G major, use Em and Bm like you're supposed to but also use E and B.
  • Do not use 4 chord progressions. Use 6 or 8 chords per section.
  • Set tempo around 90 bpm
  • Your lyrics should be utter nonsense, just make it rhyme
  • Typical song structure, which Noel stole from The Smith's There Is a Light (he called it the perfect pop song):
    • Intro - 12 bars of building tension to be relieved by the verse
    • Verse
    • Verse
    • Bridge
    • Chorus
    • Verse with a solo instead of lyrics
    • Verse
    • Verse
    • Bridge
    • Chorus
    • Outro
