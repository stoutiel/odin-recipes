Blur made music intended to appeal to alternative rock fans who were put off by the drabness of grunge and shoegaze. It turned out that there was a massive market for people who liked interesting music and were also not depressed. This was Blur's formula for Britpop:

  • Use a telecaster with a vox amp and either a jazz or precision bass.
  • Guitar plays pop rock rhythms and country licks while bass does walking lines
  • Minimal effects, just a little gain. Twangy tones.
  • Drums beats are driving and danceable with fills to mark changes in the song
  • Play standard rock chord progressions but throw in a few flat chords, some majors when
    a minor is expected, use the major and minor versions of the same chord (eg D and Dm)
  • honestly just copy Beatles progressions especially from Rubber Soul
  • Write lyrics about life's simple pleasures with a lot of references to art and literature
  • Set tempo around 120 bpm
  • Typical song structure:
    • Intro
    • Verse
    • Interlude
    • Chrous
    • Interlude
    • Verse
    • Chorus
    • Interlude with a solo that uses a bunch of out-of-key notes
